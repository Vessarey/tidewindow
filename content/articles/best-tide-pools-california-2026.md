**California's tide pools run on an upside-down calendar in 2026. This week, July 15-17, holds the summer's deepest remaining minus tides — dawn lows to −1.63 ft at Pillar Point Harbor (NOAA station 9414131). But the year's deepest daylight low at all four California stations we compute lands on Christmas Eve, December 24, in the afternoon: −1.88 ft at La Jolla (9410230) and San Diego (9410170), −1.83 ft at Monterey (9413450), and −1.90 ft at Pillar Point.**

## California's four tidepool coasts

"Best tide pools in California" lists usually run the same names — La Jolla, Cabrillo, Pacific Grove, Fitzgerald — as if one tide table covers them all. It doesn't. The four cluster around four NOAA reference stations spread across 700 miles of coast, and in 2026 the differences that matter are the clock and the gate:

Read across the "when it lands" column and you can watch the tide travel: the same Christmas Eve low arrives at La Jolla at 3:47 PM and at Pillar Point at 5:10 PM, sweeping south to north up the coast over about an hour and 20 minutes. The same lag shows up in this week's dawn run — La Jolla bottoms at 4:54 AM today, Pillar Point at 6:19 AM. Southern California always gets the low first.

The south-first timing has a practical bite. At La Jolla, today's −1.51 ft low lands at 4:54 AM — more than an hour before sunrise — so only 113 minutes of its window fall in daylight, and its score is 68. At Pillar Point the low arrives 85 minutes later in the day, at 6:19 AM, catching 199 daylight minutes and scoring 82. In summer, the farther north you are on this list, the more light your window gets. The gates bite too. Fitzgerald Marine Reserve opens at 8:00 AM every day, so today's 6:19 AM low happens behind its gate — but the water stays workable there until about 9:20 AM, leaving a real post-gate hour; the neighboring [Pillar Point reef](/guides/pillar-point-tide-pools-2026/) has no gate at all. At Cabrillo National Monument the gate opens at 9:00 AM and today's window closes at 7:45 AM, so the summer run simply does not exist there — the [Cabrillo guide](/guides/cabrillo-tide-pools-2026/) covers the workaround (the same station's predictions cover the ungated Sunset Cliffs stretch up the peninsula).

## The upside-down part: 2026 peaks on Christmas Eve

At every Washington station we compute, the year's deepest daylight low came this July. California is the opposite: its July lows are pre-dawn or barely past sunrise, while its December lows arrive mid-afternoon — and December's are deeper. The result is that California's best daylight tidepooling of 2026 is Christmas week:

The south-first lag matters even more in winter. In San Diego the December lows land mid-afternoon with hours of light left — Christmas Day at La Jolla, a −1.72 ft low at 4:33 PM on a holiday, scores 90, the only window to reach the Exceptional band in any of our four stations' deep-low rankings. On the northern half of the coast the same lows drift toward dusk: Pillar Point's deepest water of the year, −1.90 ft on December 24, bottoms out at 5:10 PM, right around sunset.

And the gates flip from obstacle to non-issue. Cabrillo's 9 AM-4:30 PM tidepool hours, which erase July entirely, comfortably hold the December 22-24 lows (2:20, 3:05, and 3:50 PM) — though the park is closed on Thanksgiving Day and Christmas Day itself. Fitzgerald's winter 5:00 PM close makes December 23 (−1.86 ft at 4:22 PM) its best usable day of the year, while the deeper December 24 low misses the close by ten minutes.

## Which California coast should you pick?

- **La Jolla.** Open city shoreline, no gate, and the best scores in the state once winter comes. Shell Beach, Hospitals Reef, and False Point, all on station 9410230. Start with the [La Jolla best-dates guide](/guides/la-jolla-tide-pools-best-dates-2026/).

- **Cabrillo National Monument.** The classic San Diego tidepool field, but it runs on office hours — the usable 2026 lows are all in the last ten weeks of the year. The [Cabrillo guide](/guides/cabrillo-tide-pools-2026/) runs every ranked window through the gate-hours filter, and routes summer dawn chasers to Sunset Cliffs instead.

- **Pacific Grove.** No gates, dawn lows in July and dusk lows in December, and every inch of shoreline inside marine protected areas — the [Pacific Grove guide](/guides/pacific-grove-tide-pools-2026/) covers Point Pinos, Asilomar, Lovers Point, and which zones are strict no-take.

- **Half Moon Bay.** Two adjacent options on one station: the ungated [Pillar Point reef](/guides/pillar-point-tide-pools-2026/) and the ranger-staffed [Fitzgerald Marine Reserve](/guides/fitzgerald-marine-reserve-tide-pooling-2026/) with its 8 AM open. The deepest water in the state on Christmas Eve, if you can be there at dusk.

## How to work a window

Arrive early and follow the water out. The National Park Service's Cabrillo tidepool guidance says to visit "approximately two hours before low tide (when the tide is receding) and two hours after low tide (when the tide is coming back in)," and recommends closed-toe shoes "with good gripping soles" because the rocks "become slippery with water and algae." Take nothing: at Cabrillo, "collecting any natural items, including living and dead organisms, shells, or rocks, is strictly forbidden," and most of the other reefs here sit inside California marine protected areas with their own take rules — some full no-take, some with narrow exceptions — listed in each station guide.
